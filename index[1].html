What is DBI?
Duisburg Business Innovation drives economic growth and innovation in the Duisburg region — working at the intersection of business, city strategy, and entrepreneurship.

We are

  • An innovation and growth engine for Duisburg
  • A professional, data-driven team
  • A living system — constantly improving
  • Collaborative observers and systematisers
  • Partners to businesses, not gatekeepers

We are not

  • A bureaucratic rulebook organisation
  • Static — we evolve with the city
  • Rule setters or culture definers alone
  • Siloed — knowledge flows openly here
  • A side-project kind of team

How We Work
Five principles guide every project at DBI. They're not slogans — they're how we actually operate.
📊

Analytical

Data and facts as the foundation of every recommendation.

📐

Structured

Clear order and systematics — no guesswork in deliverables.

💡

Data-Based

Decisions backed by evidence. Opinions are inputs, not outputs.

🎯

Pragmatic

Solution-oriented and feasible. We solve real problems, practically.

📋

Presentation-Ready

Communicable and clear. If it can't be shown, it's not done.

The goal: System logic instead of one-off solutions. We build things that scale — not workarounds that need fixing next month.
Clarity of Expectations
⏰

Clear Deadlines

Agreed deadlines are kept. Flag issues early — never silently miss.

📁

Clean Documentation

Everything traceable and complete. Your future self will thank you.

📊

Structured Results

Presentable and clearly structured. Never hand over raw notes.

🔄

Regular Check-ins

Continuous exchange — proactive updates, not reactive fire-fighting.

Your First 6 Months
A phased journey from observer to contributor. Each month builds on the last.
Months 1–2
Understand — How do we really work?
Observe before you suggest. Learn the actual patterns of collaboration, not just what's written.
  • Shadow key meetings
  • Observe tool usage
  • Document friction points
  • Interview teammates
Month 3
Structure — Where do we need standards?
Identify patterns and gaps. Start connecting what you've observed to where structure would help.
  • Map communication formats
  • Analyse tool overlaps
  • Review existing documents
Month 4
Standardise — Define minimum rules
Propose lightweight standards that solve real friction — not just theoretical improvements.
  • Draft work standards
  • Propose decision frameworks
  • Validate with teammates
Month 5
Digitise — Set up the dashboard
Translate the standards into the living digital system — modular, clickable, max 2 navigation levels.
  • Build module structure
  • Populate content
  • Connect tools
Month 6
Test & Optimise — Pilot and feedback
Run the Playbook with the team. Collect real feedback and iterate — this is a living system, not a launch.
  • Pilot with new joiners
  • Collect structured feedback
  • Publish v1.0
Tools We Use
Every tool has a purpose. We aim for no duplication — if two tools do the same job, we pick one.
Principle: Use the right tool for the job — consistently. Switching between tools for the same task creates knowledge silos.
📁

Document Management

All documents live in a shared, structured folder system. Ask your lead for access in Week 1.

💬

Internal Communication

Quick questions go in the team channel. Decisions and context belong in a document.

📋

Task Management

📊

Reporting & Analytics

Data-backed presentations are the standard. Dashboards before raw spreadsheets.

🎥

Video Calls

How We Make Decisions
Unclear decision-making is one of the most common friction points in growing teams. Here's how DBI handles it.

Your role in decisions

  • Provide structured analysis
  • Surface options with trade-offs
  • Document the decision once made
  • Flag blockers early

Not your job

  • Set the rules unilaterally
  • Make strategic calls alone
  • Define culture by yourself
  • Decide without context
⚡

Day-to-Day Decisions

Own them. Small operational choices are yours to make — document the reasoning briefly.

🤝

Team Decisions

Consult affected colleagues. Use a short async brief before calling a meeting.

📈

Strategic Decisions

Bring analysis, not just questions. Proposals with evidence get decided faster.
